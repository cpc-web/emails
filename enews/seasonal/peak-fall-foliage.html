It’s finally that picturesque time of year — peak fall foliage has arrived in Central Park! Many of the Park’s 20,000 trees have turned vibrant shades of yellow, orange, and red. Visit the Park soon to catch the colors before they’re gone.

Ready to see Central Park’s changing leaves? Get started with these tips:
  • Save our fall foliage map to your phone before heading to the Park — it tells you which landscapes to visit and what trees and colors you’ll find there.
  • Brush up on the Park’s trees before you go! Learn about the Park’s most colorful species from a Conservancy arborist.
  • Share your photos with us on social media by tagging @centralparknyc and using the hashtag #CentralParkFoliageWatch.
Admire fall foliage on a guided walk

Experience the bright colors with others on a guided Fall Foliage Walk. You’ll discover easy tips for identifying trees, and learn about the Pool and the North Woods while marveling at their beautiful colors.
