Billy Johnson Playground, one of Central Park’s most beloved play spaces, is now open!

The Conservancy’s renovation of this playground at East 67th Street has preserved and reinforced its rustic character and rich history. One of the playground’s most iconic features is its 45-foot granite slide — and now there’s twice the fun! We’ve added a second, accessible slide that accommodates younger visitors.

Other additions include enhanced water features, an expanded rustic pergola with benches, new bucket swings, and the playground’s first tire swing. Read more about what’s new at Billy Johnson Playground.

 

 

Curious about other restoration projects in Central Park? Here’s a roundup of some of the other work going on in the Park:

Belvedere Castle: Our restoration of the Belvedere, reopening in 2019, includes addressing the overall condition of its structures and terraces, modernizing the systems that support its preservation and use, and restoring lost aspects of its historic design.

Safari Playground: Safari Playground’s reconstruction maintains its beloved theme while introducing new and enhanced play features and improving accessibility. The playground will reopen this winter.

East 96th Playground: Our reconstruction of the East 96th Street Playground will enhance play spaces to better serve a wide range of ages, improve accessibility, and add a picnic area. The playground will reopen in 2019.

The Central Park Conservancy raises the Park’s nearly $80 million annual operating budget and is responsible for the work essential to keeping Central Park beautiful.
