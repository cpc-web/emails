Central Park began as an idea, and grew into a reality with the planting of its first tree. Now, over 20,000 trees, encompassing more than 170 species, thrive. They are, without a doubt, the beating heart of our beloved Park and the City — they purify the air we breathe, give habitat to our favorite woodland creatures, and provide a sense of calm and serenity that so many find indispensable in this bustling metropolis.

And with careful upkeep, many of these trees can exist for hundreds of years. Some, like the grove of American elms lining the Mall, will outlive us all.
