, Hello,

I want to share something personal with you: my fondest memory of Central Park…

I’ll always cherish strolling the Mall when my daughter was a baby, pushing her carriage beneath the leafy canopy. Although I love the escape to the Ramble and my daily runs past the Reservoir, the Mall has captured my heart because of its big, beautiful American elm trees.

The rustle of the leaves in the wind, the shade they provide on a sunny day, the dappled light on the ground as the sunlight shines through… It’s easy to get lost in splendor of the Park’s trees — and even more amazing when you learn there are 20,000 of them!

That’s why I have a little quiz for you today. Each year, we plant new trees to ensure the Park remains vibrant and beautiful. How many trees do you think we planted last year?

Can you guess how many trees we planted in 2017?

Caring for so many trees is no small feat, especially when there are over 170 species of trees in the Park. Dedicated Conservancy professionals spend hundreds of hours pruning, mulching, and inspecting each and every tree in the Park. It takes a lot of hard work from our diligent arborists and grounds crew to keep our trees healthy all year long.

Perhaps you’ve marveled at the delicate cherry blossoms and their ethereal dance in spring. Or maybe you’ve enjoyed resting beneath an English oak on a hot day, escaping the scorching summer sun in their cool shade. Maybe now, as temperatures cool, you’ll enjoy hearing your footsteps crunch through fallen red maple leaves as you admire the brilliant glory of autumn.

Truly, the blaze of orange, red and gold of Central Park’s trees in the fall — it is a sight unlike any other.

Each beautiful season, which brings millions of visitors to the Park, takes its toll. That’s why dedicated Conservancy groundskeepers plant new saplings and trees all throughout the Park every year. It’s a big task and an important part of ensuring our Park remains the verdant and welcoming oasis we all know and love.

If you find as much joy from our Park’s trees as I do, I hope you’ll help to keep them thriving by making a gift to the Conservancy. Your support means all of the Park will continue to be a much needed respite from our hectic city — no matter the season.

Thank you for everything you do for Central Park.

With kind regards, 


Elizabeth W. Smith
President & CEO, Central Park Conservancy
