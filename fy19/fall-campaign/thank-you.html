Dear Friend of the Park,

I’m so grateful for your love of Central Park.

On behalf of all of the Central Park Conservancy staff, thank you. Thank you for joining us to celebrate and honor the 160th anniversary of the first tree planted in Central Park.

I thought you might like to know that because you and others stepped up and rushed special gifts, we were able to raise over $45,000 to protect and preserve the legacy of Central Park.

Your support means we’ll be able to continue to plant trees, restore playgrounds, tend fields, and do even more to keep Central Park clean, beautiful, and open to the public every single day.

With winter just around the corner, there is much to be done to prepare the Park for the colder weather, and I rest easy knowing that the Conservancy can rely on the generosity of this special community to make it all happen.

, I hope you’ll have the chance to make it out to Central Park this fall in time to view the foliage — if you haven’t already, I highly recommend bringing a copy of our brand new Fall Guide with you.

With kind regards, 


Elizabeth W. Smith
President & CEO, Central Park Conservancy

The Central Park Conservancy raises 75% of Central Park's annual budget and is responsible for the day-to-day maintenance and operations that keep the park beautiful.
